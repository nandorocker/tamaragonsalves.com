Plain-English Plan

Contact Page And Form

A short version of the contact form design for Tamara's website.

What We Are Building

We will add a contact form so visitors can send Tamara a message from the website.

The form will appear in two places: a full contact page and a smaller version on the homepage.

Where It Will Live

  • A full page at /en/contact and /pt/contact.
  • A compact form in the homepage contact section.

The menu's Contact link will go to the new contact page.

What The Form Asks For

The form will ask for name, email, inquiry type, and message.

Visitors can also add organization, phone, and preferred language if they want.

Inquiry types will include speaking, consulting or training, media interviews, legal or policy collaboration, academic or research work, and other messages.

What Visitors See

The intro will stay short and welcoming. It will say that visitors can use the form for invitations, interviews, collaborations, training, consulting, and related inquiries.

The page will also keep Instagram, LinkedIn, and direct email as contact options.

The email address will still be added by JavaScript so it does not sit in the HTML.

Privacy Text

There will be no privacy checkbox.

Instead, the form will show this text near the send button:

By sending this message, you confirm that you have read and accept the Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.

The Privacy Policy and Terms links will use the visitor's current language.

After Someone Sends A Message

The form will disappear and show this confirmation:

Thank you for reaching out. Your message has been sent to Tamara, and she will review it soon.

A small text link will let them return to the form if they need to send another message.

What Happens Behind The Scenes

The website will send the message through Resend to info@tamaragonsalves.com.

Tamara can reply directly because the visitor's email will be used as the reply-to address.

The site will not save contact messages in a database. It will only email them.

Spam Protection

The form will include a hidden field that real visitors will not fill out. Bots often fill hidden fields, so the server can reject those messages.

The server will also reject messages that are too long.

How We Will Check It

Tests will cover missing fields, bad email addresses, spam-field rejection, and the email sent through Resend.

Before calling the work done, we will run bun test and bun run build.
